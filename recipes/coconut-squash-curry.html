Coconut Squash Curry

Description

This curry recipe is a delicious rich tomato based curry dish. In this recipe we like to use pumpkin when it’s in season but you can easily use sweet potato or butternut squash and make it any time of year. We like to leave the skin on our veg where possible as thats where lots of the fiber is. Curry recipes like many Indian dishes uses a lot of spices to give a great depth of flavor but don’t worry if you don’t have all the ground spices, you can simply use curry powder and a bit of chili if you like. We love this recipe as it’s highly adaptable to whatever you have in the fridge and cupboard, simply use your favorite veg, cooked beans, and any leafy greens you like.

Ingredients

  • 400 g butterbeans
  • 400 g chickpeas
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 5 cm piece of fresh ginger
  • 1/2 tsp chili flakes
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 2 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 400 g full fat coconut milk
  • 650 g squash / pumpkin
  • 90 g spinach
  • 15 g fresh coriander / cilantro / herb of choice

Steps

  1. Drain and rinse the butter beans and chickpeas or cooked beans of choice. Roughly chop the fresh coriander or herb of choice
  2. Blend or finely chop the onion, ginger, garlic, add to the pan with chilli flakes and ½ tsp of salt, cook on medium until onions are soft and start to lightly brown, this shold take about 5 mins. Add the chopped pumpkin / squash along with a generous pinch of salt, add 1-2 tbsp of water, cover the pan with a tight fitting lid, cook for 5-10 mins until the pumpkin /squash has started to cook through. If the pumpkin or squash does start to stick add another 1-2tbsp of water and loosen it from the pan.
  3. Add the tin of chopped tomatoes, the tin of coconut milk, all the spices, 200ml of water, the remaining ½ tsp of salt, cook for 5 mins, stirring occasionally, finally add the baby spinach and stir through to wilt, garnish with fresh coriander and serve with your favorite cooked grain or Naan bread.
  4. Enjoy!
